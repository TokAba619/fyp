What is Google Cloud?

Google Cloud is a service by Google that lets people and businesses use powerful computers over the internet to store files, run websites, build apps, and keep data safe, without needing to buy or manage their own hardware.














What is Storage Bucket?

A Google Cloud Storage bucket is a container used to store and organize files like images, documents, and backups on the Google Cloud Platform. It's scalable, secure, and accessible online. However, if not properly configured, it can expose sensitive data to the public, leading to serious security risks.














Real Cases Happen in World

Misconfigured Google Cloud buckets can expose sensitive data and cause serious risks. Without proper security, companies can face financial loss, legal issues, and damage to their reputation.

Google Cloud leak linked to Shark Tank contestant exposes 83,000

In April 2024, a misconfigured GCS bucket linked to Alice’s Table exposed over 37,000 files containing personally identifiable information (PII) of more than 83,000 customers, including names, email addresses, and home addresses

Ophion Security found misconfigured Google Cloud buckets that exposed sensitive data.

In December, we identified and reported misconfigurations on numerous sensitive Google Cloud storage buckets that contained millions of sensitive user data.

Google Cloud Buckets Exposed in Rampant Misconfiguration

Comparitech surveyed 2,064 Google Cloud buckets and found 131 were open to unauthorized access. Exposed data included 6,000 scanned documents like passports and birth certificates from children in India, and a Russian developer’s database with email server credentials and chat logs.

Thousands of Android and iOS Apps Leak Data From the Cloud

Zimperium’s 2021 report found that many mobile apps had hard-coded Google Cloud credentials, leading to data leaks like chat messages, medical info, and device IDs. About 14% of cloud-based apps had serious security flaws.

WXobin leak: used AI to screen job applicants but storing personal data in an open bucket

HR tech company Xobin misconfigured a Google Cloud bucket, exposing data of over 523,000 job applicants. The leaked files included resumes, IDs, passports, contact details, and assessments. The bucket had over 47 million files and stayed public for months before being found.

Massive data leak: 200 billion files exposed in cloud buckets

An AI startup called WotNot, based in India, accidentally exposed 346,381 files through a misconfigured GCS bucket with no authentication required. The data included passport scans, medical records, resumes, travel itineraries, and more.
